Cuban coffee (Cafecito)

Cuban coffee is sweet, rich, and delicious. Traditionally, it's served after dinner, and there's no need for dessert when you're sharing this beverage with family and friends. It requires a special technique that creates shots of espresso with a thick layer of foamy crema on top.

To make Cuban coffee, a 6-cup or 9-cup stovetop espresso maker or moka pot work best. Brewing the coffee is easy and fine-ground Cuban coffee makes it authentic. Freshly ground coffee beans work just as well.

New Orleans Cafe Noir

Cafe noir is a delicious staple of New Orleans cuisine. Although less famous than gumbo or etouffee, the spiced beverage is worth a trip to the city itself, as its fragrant aroma and warming flavor make it a delightful break alongside a hot serving of crispy and sugary beignets.

A Flat white is an espresso drink made with a shot of espresso and two shots of steamed milk. It takes a little bit of skill to prepare, but once you've mastered this recipe, it's easy to make Flat white espresso drinks again and again.
